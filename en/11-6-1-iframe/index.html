metadata_fields.definicion

Urban waste collected per capita and proportion of urban waste treated (incinerated, landfilled, and recycled) in relation to total urban waste generated. Urban waste is considered to be waste from households and the service sector (businesses, offices, and public or private institutions), the management of which is assumed by local authorities.
metadata_fields.formula

Urban waste collected per capita

\[RURP^{t} = \frac{RUR^{t}}{P^{t}}\]

where:

$RUR^{t} =$ amount of urban waste collected in year $t$

$P^{t} =$ population as of 1 July of year $t$


Proportion of urban waste collected and managed in controlled facilities

\[PRUR^{t} = \frac{RUG^{t}}{RUR^{t}} \cdot 100\]

where:

$RUG^{t} =$ amount of urban waste generated in year $t$

$RUR^{t} =$ amount of urban waste collected and managed in controlled facilities in year $t$


Proportion of urban waste treated, per treatment operation

\(PRU_{treatment}^{t} = \frac{RU_{treatment}^{t}}{RU^{t}} \cdot 100\)

$RU_{treatment}^{t} =$ amount of urban waste treated (by type of treatment) in year $t$

$RUR^{t} =$ amount of urban waste collected and managed in controlled facilities in year $t$

metadata_fields.justificacion_global

Urban households and businesses produce substantial amounts of solid waste that must be collected regularly, recycled or treated and disposed properly in order to maintain healthy and sanitary living conditions.

Many cities are increasingly facing solid waste management challenges due to rapid urbanization, lack of technical and financial capacity or low policy priority. In addition, the higher the income level of a city, the greater the amount of the solid waste produced.

Therefore, the economic growth to be experienced in the developing and emerging countries will pose greater challenges in solid waste management to local governments in the next decades.

Source: United Nations Statistics Division
